music programming

Going down the rabbit hole of programming languages for music.
Recently I have been getting into ChucK and Max for making music. This is something that I feel will hopefully break open the restrictive nature of my traditional music making processes. When working with something like Max you are forced to think about music differently. Things like BPM, time signatures and scales are foreign concepts to Max and require specific definition of what they are.
What I mean is, you are in full control of the discrete elements that make up larger systems. This allows you to create systems that are wired how you want, and not how they are wired traditionally.
The result of this, I have found so far, is easier exploration of unique sonic experiences.
